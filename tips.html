Security Best Practices

Follow these guidelines to stay one step ahead of cybercriminals.

Enable MFA

Always use Multi-Factor Authentication. Even if a scammer gets your password, they can't access your account without the second code.

Inspect Links

Hover over links before clicking. Scammers often use "bit.ly" or misspelled domains like "g00gle.com" to trick you.

Ignore Urgency

Scams rely on "Fear of Missing Out" or panic. If a message says "Act now or your account will be deleted," it is likely a fraud.

Update Software

Keep your phone and browser updated. Security patches fix the vulnerabilities that hackers use to steal data.

Verify Sender Identity

Always check who sent the message. If it’s from an unknown number or suspicious email, don’t trust it. Official organizations usually use verified contact information.

Use Security Software

Install reputable antivirus and anti-malware apps on your devices. They can detect and block scam messages before they reach you.
